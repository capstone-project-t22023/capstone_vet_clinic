F.A.Q

Frequently Asked Questions

PawsomeVet Clinic offers a wide range of veterinary services for your beloved pets, including wellness exams, vaccinations, spaying/neutering, dental care, surgery, internal medicine, diagnostic imaging, and emergency care.

We recommend bringing your pet in for a wellness exam at least once a year. For senior pets or those with ongoing medical conditions, more frequent visits may be necessary. Regular check-ups help us detect potential health issues early and keep your pet in optimal health.

To make your pet's visit as stress-free as possible, ensure they are safely contained in a carrier or on a leash. Bring any previous medical records if available, along with a list of current medications and their diet. Also, try to keep your pet calm and relaxed during the visit.

Yes, we provide emergency veterinary care during our regular business hours. If your pet needs urgent medical attention after-hours, we recommend contacting the nearest 24-hour emergency animal hospital.

In most cases, we prefer the pet owner to be present during procedures and treatments. However, we understand that circumstances may not always allow this. Please discuss your situation with our staff, and we will do our best to accommodate your needs.

If you suspect your pet has ingested something toxic or is experiencing a medical emergency, contact us immediately during business hours. For after-hours emergencies, please contact the nearest emergency animal hospital or the Australian Animal Poisons Helpline.
