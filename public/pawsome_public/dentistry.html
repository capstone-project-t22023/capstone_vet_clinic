Dentistry

Dentistry

Dental Checks

  1. The oral health of your pet has a significant impact on their overall health.
  2. Dental illness frequently causes substantial discomfort, but because pets often learn to deal with it and don't complain, there may be no evident indications at home. Furthermore, oral disease can transport hazardous bacteria to your pet's heart, liver, and kidneys.
  3. Our vets offer free dental exams to examine your pet's dental health and create a customised dental treatment plan, which may involve in-clinic treatment (teeth cleaning, dental xrays, and extractions if necessary).
  4. We provide a variety of dental care items, such as specific dental meals, dental chews, pet toothpaste, and pet toothbrushes. This means we are a one-stop shop for all of your pet's dental requirements!

Dental Cleaning

Pets require regular dental cleaning just like humans do. But because our patients wonâ€™t sit in a chair with their mouths open, we do have to anaesthetise them to allow for a comprehensive exam and thorough cleaning. Most pets go home the same day as their dental cleaning. The procedure involves:

  1. Complete oral exam with evaluation of each tooth for periodontal disease, tooth mobility and fractures
  2. Digital dental x-rays to assess tooth root and jaw bone health that is not visible from the outside
  3. Ultrasonic scaling to remove all traces of tartar from the teeth and below the gum line
  4. High-speed polishing to return the enamel to a smooth surface (that is less inclined to accumulate tartar)
  5. Dental surgery where necessary.
  6. Ongoing instructions for home dental care options with the goal to increase the time between dental cleanings.

Dental Radiology

We can see your pet's tooth roots below the gum line and into the jaw bone thanks to digital dental radiology. This enables us to detect bone loss, infection, or hidden disease that may be causing your pet pain but is not obvious during a visual oral exam.

Dental Surgery

There are various reasons why a pet can need dental surgery (tooth extraction). These are some examples:


  1. Advanced periodontitis has resulted in irreparable bone loss around the tooth.
  2. Broken teeth that expose the pulp cavity (which contains the tooth nerve - this is painful!)
  3. Tooth resorption, which is most common in cats, is a disorder in which the tooth enamel erodes, exposing the dental nerve and causing excruciating agony.

We offer pain management before, during, and after your pet's dental operation.
