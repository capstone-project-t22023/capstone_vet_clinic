Poison Guide

Poison Guide

At PawsomeVet, we understand that your pet's safety is your top priority. That's why we've created this comprehensive Poison Guide to help you be prepared in case of accidental exposure to harmful substances. While we hope you never have to use this guide, having this knowledge on hand can make all the difference in protecting your beloved furry friends.

    1. Immediate Action:

If you suspect your pet has ingested a poisonous substance, time is of the essence. Contact us at PawsomeVet immediately at [Your Clinic Phone Number] or reach out to a poison control hotline, such as the ASPCA Animal Poison Control Center at 1-888-426-4435 or the Pet Poison Helpline at 1-800-213-6680. The sooner you seek help, the better the chances of a positive outcome.

    2. Information to Provide:

When you call for assistance, be ready to provide the following information:

  • The type of poison (if known)
  • The amount ingested (if known)
  • The time of ingestio
  • Your pet's age, weight, and breed
  • Having this information on hand can help our veterinarians and poison control experts determine the best course of action.
    3. Do's and Don'ts:

While waiting for professional guidance, here are some general guidelines:

  • Do Not Wait: Seek help immediately, even if your pet is not showing symptoms yet.
  • Do Not Induce Vomiting: Do not try to make your pet vomit unless instructed by a veterinarian or poison control expert. It can be harmful in some cases.
  • Secure the Poison: If safe, remove the source of poisoning from your pet's reach to prevent further exposure.
  • Keep Calm: Try to keep your pet calm and quiet. Stress and excitement can worsen some poisoning symptoms.
  • Do Not Offer Home Remedies: Avoid giving your pet any home remedies or over-the-counter medications unless directed by a professional.
  • Transport Safely: If advised to seek immediate veterinary care, transport your pet to our clinic safely. Use a secure carrier or leash.
